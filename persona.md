# Boostly Persona: "The Owner Who's Always There"

## Demographics
- **Age:** 35–58
- **Role:** Independent restaurant owner or operator (1–3 locations)
- **Experience:** 5–20 years in the industry, often started as a cook or manager
- **Restaurant type:** Fast casual, pizzeria, taqueria, BBQ joint, diner, family-owned sit-down
- **Works:** 55–70 hours a week. Usually there before open and after close.
- **Education:** High school or some college. Learned the business by doing it.

---

## What Their Day Looks Like
- Handling a no-show line cook at 10am
- Watching the DoorDash tablet light up while their dining room sits half-empty
- Approving a Facebook ad they're not sure is working
- Texting their best server to come in early
- Going home exhausted and doing it again tomorrow

---

## What They Care About
- Keeping regulars coming back — that's their whole business model
- Filling slow nights (Tuesday, early week) without running deep discounts
- Not getting killed by delivery app commissions (25–30% per order)
- Knowing which promotions actually work vs. which ones just cost money
- Running lean — they can't afford tools that require a dedicated person to manage

---

## What They Like
- Simple tools that don't need training
- Seeing real numbers: "you sent X texts, Y people came in, made $Z"
- Set-it-and-forget-it automation
- Things that work without bothering their staff
- Other restaurant owners vouching for something (peer trust is high)

---

## What They Dislike
- Salespeople overpromising
- Complicated dashboards they'll never open
- Paying for something and not knowing if it's working
- Tools that add work for their team
- Contracts and hidden fees

---

## Problems They're Trying to Solve
1. **Guests walk out and never come back** — no way to reach them after they leave
2. **DoorDash owns their customer relationships** — 30% commission, zero data
3. **Slow nights bleed money** — no way to drive traffic on demand
4. **Marketing spend is a black box** — boosted posts, Yelp ads, no idea what's working
5. **They're already stretched thin** — any new tool has to run itself

---

## Objections That Would Stop Them From Signing Up
1. "Will my customers actually give their number?"
2. "My staff is already slammed — they can't add one more thing"
3. "Is $597/month worth it for a place doing $80K a month?"
4. "I've tried email marketing before. Nobody reads it."
5. "How long does setup take? I don't have time for a project right now."
6. "What if it doesn't work for my type of restaurant?"

---

## Mindset
They're not looking for marketing software. They're looking for more regulars and a way to fill empty tables without cutting their margins. If you can show them the math and make setup sound easy, they'll buy. If you can't, they'll scroll past.
